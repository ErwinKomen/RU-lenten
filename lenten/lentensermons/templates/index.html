The LentenSermons web application provides search facilities to the main late medieval Lenten sermon collections. under construction

Project

The “Lenten sermon bestsellers” project, led by Dr. P. Delcorno [link to web-page], aims to map and provide several tools to navigate to the most popular Lenten sermon collections circulating in late medieval Europe. Lenten preaching played a vital role in process of mass-communication and identity formation as an intensified form of religious instruction. The web-application aims to facilitate the identification and analysis of the conceptual and textual ‘infrastructure’ that supported this widespread socio-religious practice.

In its first phase, the project will map the Lenten sermon collections printed before the Reformation (1470-1520), considering these normative texts as pocket-sized encyclopaedias that reflected and produced the shared (religious) grammar of the time.

Getting started

The LentenSermon application has been designed by the Technical Service Group of the Humanities Lab at the Radboud University Nijmegen. Any researcher is invited to register (via sign up). Regular researchers are able to read and search through the information that is provided.
